My interest in code started when I was in high school. I created my own website (thanks, Angelfire!) where all I needed to do was pick my favorite color and add some words to fit in to a predefined template. I quickly became curious how things were working under the hood and I wanted to have more control. Then began my self-teaching of HTML and CSS (inline at the time) and it was a project I maintained and was proud of. However, college came and I somehow didn't think to pursue it, and consequently forgot all about it.

Skip to the present. In 2014 I randomly came across an article that reminded me of those good times. In 2015 I decided to take a leap of faith, quit my job and attend a coding bootcamp full time. Best decision I've made in a long, long time. I was a student at General Assembly during the summer/fall of 2015 in their Web Development Immersive program. In twelve short weeks I learned a whole gamut of information. HTML and CSS, JavaScript, then jumping to Ruby, Ruby on Rails, Active Record, MVC frameworks, agile methodology, just to name a few.  It was the most intensive and compelling learning experience imaginable, and it was more than I could have hoped for.
