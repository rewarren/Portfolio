My interest in code started when I was in high school. I created my own website (thanks, Angelfire!) where all I needed to do was pick my favorite color and add some words to fit in to a predefined template. I quickly became curious how things were working under the hood and I wanted to have more control. Then began my self-teaching of HTML and CSS (inline at the time) and it was a project I maintained and was proud of. When college came, though, I followed a different pursuit, and consequently forgot all about it.

Skip to the near present. In 2014, I read an article about a woman who made a complete career change after learning how to code. The article resonated with me because it brought back all the good feelings that I associated with my own experience, and inspired me to think about my career and my interests. In 2015 I decided to take a leap of faith, quit my job and attend a coding bootcamp full time. Great decision on my part, if I may say so myself. I was a student at General Assembly during the summer/fall of 2015 in their Web Development Immersive program in Washington, DC. In twelve short weeks I learned a whole gamut of information. HTML and CSS, JavaScript, then jumping to Ruby, Ruby on Rails, Active Record, MVC frameworks, agile methodology, just to name a few.  It was an intensive and compelling learning experience, and turned out to be more than I could have hoped for. You can check them out here.
